### RAID Arrays

- **RAID 0 (Striping)**
  - Minimum: 2 drives  
  - Splits data across multiple drives for performance  
  - No fault tolerance

- **RAID 1 (Mirroring)**
  - Minimum: 2 drives  
  - Duplicates data across drives  
  - High redundancy, lower usable capacity

- **RAID 5 (Striping with Parity)**
  - Minimum: 3 drives  
  - Data + parity distributed across drives  
  - Can survive 1 drive failure

- **RAID 6 (Double Parity)**
  - Minimum: 4 drives  
  - Like RAID 5 but can survive 2 drive failures

- **RAID 10 (Striping + Mirroring)**
  - Minimum: 4 drives  
  - Combines performance and redundancy  
  - Requires mirrored pairs
